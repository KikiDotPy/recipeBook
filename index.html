Struffoli

Bite-sized Neapolitan honey balls, traditionally enjoyed during Christmas, symbolizing abundance and unity. Fun fact: The name "struffoli" may derive from the Greek word "strongoulos," meaning 'round'.

Certified by Kiki

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg all-purpose flour
  • 10 eggs
  • 4 tablespoons sugar
  • 50 g butter
  • 2 packets vanilla powder (approximately 1 g)
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Pinch of salt
  • Strega liqueur and grappa, to taste
  • 400 g honey (wildflower or acacia)
  • Colorful sprinkles ("diavolilli")
  • Oil for frying (extra virgin olive oil or peanut oil)

Instructions:

  1. On a work surface, form a well with the flour. Add eggs, sugar, melted butter, vanilla powder, citrus zests, salt, and a splash of Strega liqueur and grappa. Mix until a smooth, compact dough forms.
  2. Cover the dough with a clean cloth and let it rest for about an hour to reduce foam during frying.
  3. Divide the dough into portions. On a floured surface, roll each portion into thin ropes. Cut into small pieces, about 1 cm each, to form the struffoli.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry the struffoli in batches until golden. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  5. In a large saucepan, warm the honey over moderate heat until it becomes liquid and starts to bubble. Add the fried struffoli and gently stir to coat them evenly.
  6. Transfer the honey-coated struffoli to a serving plate, shaping them as desired (commonly into a mound or ring). Decorate with colorful sprinkles.

Mostaccioli

Traditional Neapolitan spiced cookies, often enjoyed during the holidays. Fun fact: Their name comes from the Latin "mustaceum," referring to cakes made with grape must.

Certified by Kiki

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 200g sugar
  • 200g honey
  • 100ml water
  • 30g unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
  • Grated zest of 1 orange
  • 8g baking ammonia or 1 tsp baking powder
  • 200g dark chocolate (for glazing)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, cinnamon, and cloves.
  2. Add the grated orange zest and baking ammonia (or baking powder) to the dry ingredients.
  3. Heat the honey and water in a small saucepan until warm, then pour it into the dry ingredients. Mix until a dough forms.
  4. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead until smooth. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  5. Roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1 cm (0.4 inches). Cut into diamond or oval shapes using a knife or cookie cutter.
  6. Preheat your oven to 180Â°C (350Â°F). Place the cut dough pieces on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 10-12 minutes.
  7. Let the baked cookies cool completely.
  8. Melt the dark chocolate in a bain-marie or microwave. Dip each cookie into the chocolate to glaze, then place on a wire rack to set.
  9. Once the chocolate is fully set, the Mostaccioli are ready to serve!

Carbonara

A Roman classic featuring pasta, eggs, Pecorino Romano, and guanciale. Fun fact: Despite its traditional roots, carbonara's origins are debated, with some suggesting it became popular post-World War II.

Certified by Kiki

Ingredients:

  • 200g Spaghetti no. 3
  • 2 whole eggs
  • 2-3 slices of guanciale (or high-quality bacon)
  • 1 additional egg yolk (optional, for extra creaminess)
  • 70g finely grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 20g finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • White wine, for deglazing

Instructions:

  1. Cut the guanciale into strips or cubes and brown slowly in a pan over low heat until crispy, being careful not to burn it.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano, Parmesan, and black pepper to taste to create the egg mixture.
  3. Cook the pasta in boiling water until al dente. While the pasta cooks, remove the guanciale from the pan and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pan.
  4. Deglaze the pan with white wine, allowing the alcohol to evaporate completely.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the pan with the guanciale fat and wine. Toss over medium-low heat, adding a little pasta cooking water to help the pasta absorb the flavors.
  6. Remove the pan from heat (or keep on a very low flame) and add the egg mixture, stirring constantly to combine without overcooking the eggs.
  7. Once well combined, add the guanciale back to the pan, stir briefly, and serve immediately with an additional sprinkling of Pecorino or Parmesan cheese.

RagÃ¹:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil and brown the pork ribs. Add the veal in large pieces and brown as well.

Almond Caramel Coffee

A staple of Italian mornings, this strong and aromatic coffee is brewed using the iconic stovetop moka pot. Associated with leisurely family breakfasts, itâ€™s a comforting ritual for many. Fun fact: The moka pot was invented in 1933 by Alfonso Bialetti and remains a symbol of Italian coffee culture worldwide.

Certified by Kiki

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 tablespoons of maple syrup (or malt syrup or honey)
  • 1 tablespoon of almond butter
  • 1 teaspoon of mesquite or ground cinnamon
  • Coffee (filtered, American, moka or barley coffee)

Instructions:

  1. To make the caramel: In a bowl, mix the almond butter and the syrup continuously until a caramel consistency is achieved. Add mesquite or cinnamon powder as desired. This caramel can be used in many preparations.
  2. To make the coffee: Prepare coffee for two people using a moka or your preferred method. Pour the coffee into a blender (if using moka coffee, add 200 ml of hot water). Add one tablespoon of the almond caramel and blend until a soft cream forms on the surface.
  3. Add a sprinkle of cinnamon on top, if desired.

Christmas Spritz

The iconic Venetian aperitivo, a sparkling blend of Prosecco, Aperol (or Campari), and soda water. Traditionally enjoyed with cicchetti (small bites), itâ€™s the perfect drink to kick off a lively evening. Fun fact: The Spritz dates back to the Austro-Hungarian Empire, when soldiers diluted Italian wine with soda!

Certified by Kiki

Ingredients:

  • 200 ml Prosecco or sparkling wine
  • 100 ml fresh orange juice
  • 100 ml cranberry juice
  • 100 ml Aperol
  • 100 ml sparkling water
  • 4 sprigs of rosemary
  • 4 slices of orange
  • Cranberries or red currants
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. Cut the oranges to extract fresh juice, keeping aside a few round slices for garnish.
  2. Fill the glasses with ice.
  3. Add the cranberry juice, orange juice, Aperol, and Prosecco or sparkling wine.
  4. Finish with a splash of sparkling water.
  5. Stir gently to mix the ingredients.
  6. Garnish with a slice of orange, cranberries or red currants, and a sprig of rosemary.
